Revealing travel patterns and city structure with taxi trip data
Based on taxi-trip data from Shanghai, we built spatially embedded networks to model intra-city spatial interactions and study urban structure. The community detection method is applied to reveal sub-regional structures, and several network measures are used to examine the properties of sub-regions. Considering the differences between long- and short-distance trips, we reveal a two-level hierarchical polycentric city structure in Shanghai. Land uses of city centers and their influence on travel patterns are also explored.
with Li Gong, Yongxi Gong, Yu Liu
